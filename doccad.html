Fusion 360 Documentation

Simple & Elegant Six-Day Autodesk Fusion 360 Documentation

Day 1: Introduction & Basics
07.04.2025 (1.5 hrs)

Started with Fusion 360 installation, interface overview, and parametric modeling fundamentals. Focus on workspace navigation and simple geometry.

Focus Areas

Navigation Bar, Timeline: Getting comfortable with the interface.
Basic Operations: Shell and cut commands for 3D shapes.

Day 2: Sketching Essentials
08.04.2025 (1.5 hrs)

Learned precise 2D sketching using constraints and dimensions and transforming sketches to 3D parts via extrusion.

Focus Areas

Sketch Tools: Lines, circles, rectangles, mirrors.
Constraints: Coincident, equal, horizontal/vertical.
3D Features: Extrusions and basic documentation.

Day 3: Intermediate & Reference Modeling
10.04.2025 (4 hrs)

Integrated reference images with scaling and advanced organic/mechanical modeling techniques using revolve, hole creation, and appearances.

Focus Areas

Canvas Images: Accurate reference for modeling.
Organic Shapes: Pikachu keychain project.
Mechanical Parts: Precise piston and connecting rod.

Day 4: Advanced Assemblies & Cloud
14.04.2025 (4 hrs)

Focused on cloud file management, complex assembly modeling, and motion mechanics including joints and parent-child component relations.

Focus Areas

Cloud Storage: Project version control.
Assembly Design: Bodies vs components.
Motion: Animated joints and constraints.

Day 5: Practice & Advanced Tools
16.04.2025 (1.5 hrs)

Reinforced skills and explored loft, pipe, coil creation, chamfer, fillet, text engraving, and McMaster-Carr integration for standard parts.

Focus Areas

Advanced Geometry: Loft and coil tools.
Finishing: Chamfer, fillets, engravings.
Parts Library: McMaster-Carr access.

Day 6: Review & Future Paths
17.04.2025 (1.5 hrs)

Collaborative problem solving, knowledge validation, and planning for advanced skill growth with simulations and sheet metal design.

Focus Areas

Troubleshooting: Constraint and assembly challenges.
Future Learning: FEA simulation and CAM.
Project Ideas: Mechanical assemblies and product models.
